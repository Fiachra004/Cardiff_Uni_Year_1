TempleOS

TempleOS was developed by Terry after his spiritual awakening it started as being called J operating systems from 2004 to 2005, LoseThose from 2006 to 2012 and SparrrowOS in late 2012. IT would finally get its name TempleOS in early 2013. TempleOS is an Operating System that was written in a language developed by Davis called Holy C which was a mix between C and C++. TempleOS was developed over the course of a decade, and by the end it was written with over 100,000 lines of code. The reason for its creation was to recreate an environment that is similar to that of the commodore 64.

Davis is quoted saying the main purpose of "LoseThose" (its name at the time) was for "making video games". Davis would later say that he was in communicaiton with god, and he was told to build an operating system, however many people put this down to the decline in Terrys mental health at that point. TempleOS has many biblical references, such as the games like "after eygpt" that are built into the system and in the mind of Terry acted as an interface with god by producing random text that would be similar to that of somone speaking in tongues.

Davis had explicitly said that some of the systems features were instructions from god such as the resolution and the colour depth. He used the system to ask questions to god about a variety of things including War, death dinosaurs, video games, cars, national anthems, bands and other things. In 2012 TempleOS was belived to be downloaded over 10,000 times, however Davis's own mental termoil had prevented him from beliveing that there was "no evidence anyone had installed it" although that there is much evidence online to dispute this as there are recordings of people using the operating system teaching others how to use it.
