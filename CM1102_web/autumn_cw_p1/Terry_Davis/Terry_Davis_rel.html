It is widely aknowledged by many of the people that followed Terry Davis's development of templeOS that it was no easy undertaking. To put it into perspective, the GEOS which was the operating system used by the Commodore 64, the system that was an inspiration for the apperance of TempleOS was built by a team of 8 programmers from Berkeley Softwarks, not to mention the fact that he developed his own version of C, "holy C" whilst creating the OS. In order to attempt any of these projects, you need to be in the high level of not only computer programming, but have a deep understanding of computer architecture.

As far as modern signifigance is concerned, Terrys work is unfortunately insignificant despite his own delusions of grandeur. The importance of his work is deeper than the creation however, as it shows the perservirence of man who was struggling deep mental issues, even when you could argue he was mentally his weakest, he was still able to express his intelligence in a way in which many people would not be able to comprahend.
